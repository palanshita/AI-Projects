What is Dementia?

Dementia is a chronic and progressive neurological disorder that affects memory, thinking, behavior, and the ability to perform everyday activities. It results from various diseases and conditions, including Alzheimer's disease, and significantly impairs a person's ability to function independently, impacting their social and occupational life.

How can we prevent it?

Preventing dementia involves regular exercise, a healthy diet, mental stimulation, quality sleep, social engagement, managing cardiovascular risk factors, and avoiding smoking and excessive alcohol. These lifestyle choices support brain health and reduce the risk of cognitive decline.

Interesting facts about Dementia

Dementia affects over 55 million people worldwide. People with bilingual abilities may develop dementia symptoms later than monolinguals. Music therapy can enhance memory and mood in dementia patients. Some forms of dementia are reversible if caused by vitamin deficiencies or infections.
